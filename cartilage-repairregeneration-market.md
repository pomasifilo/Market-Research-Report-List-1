<p><p>The cartilage repair and regeneration market is divided into two main types: cell-based and non-cell-based solutions. Cell-based therapies involve the use of living cells, such as chondrocytes or stem cells, to regenerate damaged cartilage, promoting natural healing processes. In contrast, non-cell-based methods include scaffolds, biomaterials, and injectable formulations that facilitate repair without using live cells. Both approaches aim to restore joint function and alleviate pain, catering to different patient needs and technological advancements in regenerative medicine.</p></p>
