<p><p>The injectable drugs market comprises various types of containers, each serving specific purposes. Ampoules are sealed glass containers that protect sensitive substances from contamination and are typically used for single-use medications. Vials are versatile containers, often designed for multiple doses, sealed with rubber stoppers and aluminum caps. Cartridges are pre-filled cylindrical containers commonly used in autoinjectors for convenient administration. Bottles provide bulk storage for solutions and can be used for both single and multiple doses, catering to diverse therapeutic needs.</p></p>
