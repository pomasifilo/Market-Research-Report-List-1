Active detection systems utilize real-time image processing to identify smoke or fire by analyzing video feeds, allowing for immediate alerts and quick response times. In contrast, passive detection systems rely on pre-recorded video analysis or sensor data to trigger alerts based on detected anomalies, providing a less instantaneous response. Both systems aim to enhance fire safety and improve response strategies in various environments.</p></p>
