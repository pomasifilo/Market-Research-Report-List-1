<p><p>The antifreeze and coolants market primarily comprises three types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is widely used in automotive and industrial applications due to its excellent thermal properties and low cost, but it is toxic. Propylene glycol is a safer alternative, often used in food and pharmaceuticals, thanks to its non-toxic nature. Glycerin, a biodegradable option, is employed in eco-friendly formulations. Each type serves specific needs, influencing market demand and application across various industries.</p></p>
