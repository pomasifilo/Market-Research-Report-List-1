<p><p>The vehicle turbocharger market consists primarily of two types: mechanical turbocharging and exhaust turbocharging. Mechanical turbocharging utilizes a belt-driven compressor to increase air intake, enhancing engine performance at low RPMs. In contrast, exhaust turbocharging harnesses exhaust gases to spin a turbine, compressing incoming air and significantly boosting power and efficiency. Both technologies aim to improve engine output and fuel economy, catering to the growing demand for more sustainable and powerful vehicle performance in the automotive industry.</p></p>
