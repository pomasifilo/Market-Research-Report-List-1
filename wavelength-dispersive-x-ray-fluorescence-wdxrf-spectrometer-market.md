<p><p>The Wavelength Dispersive X-Ray Fluorescence (WDXRF) spectrometer market can be segmented into two main types: optical and electromagnetic. Optical WDXRF systems use diffraction gratings or crystals to separate X-rays based on their wavelengths, resulting in high-resolution spectral data for elemental analysis. In contrast, electromagnetic WDXRF systems utilize advanced detectors and electronic systems to analyze the emitted X-rays. Both types serve various industries, including materials science, environmental monitoring, and quality control, offering precise elemental composition analysis and enhanced sensitivity.</p></p>
