<p><p>Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography (EUVL) is a cutting-edge technology used in semiconductor manufacturing to produce smaller, more efficient microchips. The EUVL market comprises three main segments: light sources, mirrors, and masks. Light sources generate the required extreme ultraviolet radiation for lithography. Mirrors are specialized optics that focus and direct this radiation onto the wafer. Masks, which contain the circuit patterns, transfer these designs onto the semiconductor substrate. Together, these components enable the advancement of next-generation electronic devices.</p></p>
